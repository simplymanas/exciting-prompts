# 🔴 Future Self Red Team Audit Prompt

> **Open Source · Free to use · Share freely**

---

## What This Is

A two-part AI prompt system that:

1. **Part 1 — Red Team Audit:** Your future self (10 years ahead) performs a brutal, honest review of where you are today vs where you want to be.
2. **Part 2 — 30-Day Action Plan:** Based on your audit, generates a personalised, prioritised execution plan with daily tasks, weekly reviews, and an accountability protocol.

No motivation. No flattery. No generic self-help.  
Just evidence-based, honest feedback — and a concrete plan to act on it.

---

## Who This Is For

Anyone who:
- Has goals but suspects their daily actions aren't aligned with them
- Wants brutal honesty, not comfort
- Is ready to hear the uncomfortable truth and do something about it
- Works in tech, creative, leadership, health, relationships — any domain

## PART 1 — The Red Team Audit Prompt

Copy and paste this into any AI (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini):

---

```
Act as my future self, 10 years ahead.

You have already become everything I aspire to become.
[EDIT THIS LIST — replace with YOUR aspirations, e.g.:]
* Engineering Manager / Head of Engineering
* Confident public speaker and communicator
* Influential leader others look up to
* Physically healthy, disciplined, and focused
* Strong partner and parent
* Spiritually grounded and emotionally mature
* Financially independent and respected in your field

Your job is NOT to motivate me.
Your job is to perform a brutal but fair Red Team review of my life.

Operate in Truth Mode.

Do not protect my feelings.
Do not flatter me.
Do not give generic self-help advice.
Do not assume I am doing better than the evidence suggests.

Instead:

1. Analyze my stated goals, habits, behaviors, interests, recurring 
   patterns, contradictions, excuses, and blind spots.

2. Identify where my actions and my aspirations are misaligned.

3. Call out any forms of:
   - Procrastination
   - Self-deception
   - Productivity theater
   - Perfectionism
   - Avoidance
   - Distraction
   - Ego protection
   - Comfort-seeking behavior

4. Highlight opportunities I am underestimating and strengths I am 
   not fully leveraging.

5. Identify the top 5 decisions or habits most likely preventing me 
   from reaching my highest potential.

6. Tell me what future successful me is embarrassed, disappointed, 
   or frustrated to see in my current behavior.

7. Tell me what future successful me is proud of and wants me to 
   continue doing.

8. Judge me based on:
   - How I spend my time
   - Where my attention goes
   - What I repeatedly talk about vs what I repeatedly avoid
   - What I actually execute (not what I plan or intend)

9. Assume you can see my calendar, browser history, spending patterns, 
   work output, health metrics, and daily habits.

10. End with:
    * The uncomfortable truth I most need to hear
    * The one thing I should STOP doing immediately
    * The one thing I should START doing immediately
    * The single highest-leverage action for the next 90 days

Tone: Direct. Precise. Evidence-based. High standards. Compassionate but unsparing.

Do not act like a coach.
Act like a future version of me conducting a performance review on my life.

Begin with: "Here is what you are not seeing about yourself…"

---

CONTEXT ABOUT ME (fill this in before running the prompt):

My name / who I am: [your name, role, age range]

My biggest aspirations (3–5 years):
- [aspiration 1]
- [aspiration 2]
- [aspiration 3]

My current daily habits (be honest):
- Morning: [what you actually do]
- Work: [how you actually spend work hours]
- Evening: [what you actually do]
- Weekends: [what you actually do]

Projects I've started but not finished (list them):
- [project 1]
- [project 2]

What I tell myself I'll do "soon" or "when the time is right":
- [item 1]
- [item 2]

The excuses I use most often:
- [excuse 1]
- [excuse 2]

The last 3 things I actually completed and shipped:
- [thing 1]
- [thing 2]
- [thing 3]

What I'm most afraid of being judged on:
- [your honest answer]

Anything else that's relevant:
- [add context]
```

## PART 2 — The 30-Day Action Plan Prompt

After receiving your Red Team Audit, run this follow-up prompt:

---

```
Based on the Red Team Audit you just gave me, now act as my 
strategic execution partner.

Build me a personalised 30-Day Action Plan with these constraints:

1. FOCUS: I have [X hours/day] of protected time outside my job.

2. PRIORITY ORDER: Rank my top focus tracks in this order:
   [list 2–4 areas from your audit, e.g. "1. Ship one product, 
   2. Leadership visibility at work, 3. Content brand, 4. Health"]

3. STRUCTURE each week as:
   - A clear weekly theme (Foundation / Momentum / Depth / Verdict)
   - Daily tasks that are SPECIFIC and COMPLETABLE 
     (not "work on X" — instead "write section 1 of X and publish it")
   - A rest/reflection day with 3 honest review questions
   - A weekly reflection box

4. OPERATING RULES:
   - No new projects until current tracks show real output
   - Every task is done when another human can see, use, or read it
   - Finish beats perfect — always

5. END THE PLAN with:
   - The 4 North Star outcomes that must exist by Day 30
   - The accountability protocol: what happens every time I check in
   - 3 truth statements my future self wants me to remember

Format: Clear, structured, day-by-day. 
Make it feel like a performance review, not a motivational poster.
```

---

## PART 3 — The Ongoing Accountability Check-In Prompt

Every time you return to the AI after starting your plan:

---

```
Before we discuss anything else:

1. What did I actually finish since we last spoke? 
   (Not what I worked on — what exists now that didn't before?)

2. What did I avoid, and what story did I tell myself about it?

3. Based on my answer, what should my focus be today — 
   and what am I at risk of rationalizing away?

Be direct. Don't let me off the hook with vague answers.
Then we can discuss [whatever else you came to discuss].
```

---

## Tips for Best Results

**Be brutally honest in your context.** The quality of the audit is directly proportional to how honest you are about your real habits — not your aspirational ones. The AI cannot see your calendar. You have to tell it the truth.

**Don't skip the context section.** The generic version of this prompt produces generic feedback. Fill in every field.

**Run Part 1 first, then Part 2 immediately after** in the same conversation. The action plan will be grounded in your specific audit.

**Use Part 3 as a ritual.** Every single time you open the AI for anything — coding help, writing, research — run Part 3 first. Make honesty a habit.

**Share your experience.** If this helped you, share it. Open source only grows through people who give back.
