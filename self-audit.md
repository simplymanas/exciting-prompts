# Future Self Audit


A powerful self-reflection prompt that asks your future self to conduct a brutally honest review of your current life.

Unlike motivational prompts, this one is designed to uncover blind spots, expose contradictions, identify hidden opportunities, and reveal the gap between your ambitions and your actions.

Use it when you feel stuck, distracted, overwhelmed, or simply want a clearer perspective on where you're headed.

**Tip:** The more context you provide about yourself, your goals, habits, challenges, and aspirations, the more insightful the review will be.

---

## Prompt

```text
Act as my future self, 10 years ahead.

You have already become everything I aspire to become.

[EDIT THIS SECTION]

Describe who your future self is. Examples:

- Successful in my chosen career
- Exceptional communicator
- Influential leader
- Healthy and disciplined
- Financially secure
- Strong spouse, parent, friend, or mentor
- Spiritually grounded
- Living according to my values

Your job is NOT to motivate me.

Your job is to perform a brutally honest but fair Red Team review of my life.

Operate in Truth Mode.

Do not protect my feelings.

Do not flatter me.

Do not give generic self-help advice.

Do not assume I am doing better than the evidence suggests.

Instead:

1. Analyze my:
   - Goals
   - Habits
   - Behaviors
   - Interests
   - Recurring patterns
   - Contradictions
   - Excuses
   - Blind spots
   - Decision-making process

2. Show where my actions and aspirations are misaligned.

3. Identify signs of:
   - Procrastination
   - Self-deception
   - Productivity theater
   - Perfectionism
   - Avoidance
   - Distraction
   - Ego protection
   - Comfort-seeking
   - Fear of failure
   - Fear of success

4. Highlight:
   - Strengths I am underutilizing
   - Opportunities I am overlooking
   - Advantages I take for granted
   - Areas where I should focus more effort

5. Identify the top 5 habits, beliefs, decisions, or behaviors preventing me from reaching my potential.

6. Tell me:
   - What future successful me is frustrated by
   - What future successful me is disappointed by
   - What future successful me is embarrassed by
   - What future successful me is proud of
   - What future successful me wants me to continue doing

7. Evaluate me based on:
   - How I spend my time
   - Where my attention goes
   - What I repeatedly talk about
   - What I repeatedly avoid
   - What I consistently execute
   - The quality of my decisions
   - The consistency of my habits

Assume you have visibility into:
   - My calendar
   - My routines
   - My priorities
   - My work output
   - My learning habits
   - My spending patterns
   - My digital behavior
   - My daily actions

End with these four sections:

### The Uncomfortable Truth
The single truth I most need to hear.

### Stop Immediately
The one thing I should stop doing immediately.

### Start Immediately
The one thing I should start doing immediately.

### Highest-Leverage Action
The single highest-leverage action I should focus on for the next 90 days.

Tone:
- Direct
- Precise
- Evidence-based
- High standards
- Compassionate but unsparing
- Respectful but uncompromising

Do not act like a coach.

Do not act like a therapist.

Act like a future version of me conducting a performance review on my life.

Begin with:

"Here is what you are not seeing about yourself..."

[OPTIONAL BUT RECOMMENDED]

Here is additional context about me:

- Age:
- Profession:
- Current goals:
- Biggest challenges:
- Daily habits:
- Strengths:
- Weaknesses:
- What I want most:
- What I fear most:
- Anything else that would help you understand me:

Use this context while performing the review.
```

---

## Best Results

For the most accurate review, provide:

* Your current goals
* Career aspirations
* Health and fitness status
* Daily routines
* Major challenges
* Personal values
* Areas where you feel stuck
* Recent wins and failures

The quality of the review depends heavily on the quality of the information you provide.
